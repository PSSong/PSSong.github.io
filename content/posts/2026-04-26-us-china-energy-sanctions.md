On April 26, 2026, as the United States strengthens sanctions on China's energy sector, the restructuring of the global energy market is accelerating. These sanctions are interpreted as a strategic move to establish a new energy hegemony centered on allies, going beyond simple economic pressure on China. Through this, the U.S. aims to strengthen energy security and reshape the global energy supply chain through cooperation with its allies.

Sanctions on China's energy sector weaken China's ability to achieve energy independence while contributing to the securing of a stable supply of energy resources for the United States and its allies. This shift is expected to play a crucial role in readjusting the geopolitical balance in East Asia and the Indo-Pacific region, and in forming a new international order through the distribution and control of energy resources.

Ultimately, the energy sanctions between the U.S. and China are expected to redraw the map of the global energy market through the restructuring of energy hegemony.
