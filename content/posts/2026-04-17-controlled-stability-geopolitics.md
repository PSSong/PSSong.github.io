Over the past day, energy prices have plummeted as tensions in the Middle East region temporarily eased. However, suggestions are being raised that this phenomenon may be part of a deeper geopolitical strategy rather than a simple result of crisis resolution. While it is true that the resumption of stable operations in the Strait of Hormuz has smoothed the supply of crude oil, the underlying intention may be to create a false sense of relief in the market.

From a geopolitical perspective, such measures could be an effort to temporarily divert the international community's attention and secure long-term strategic advantages during times of crisis. While the easing of tensions in the Middle East has a positive impact on the energy market in the short term, in the long term, it may reflect an intention to manage crisis situations and maintain control over international resources. Therefore, investors and policymakers need to recognize and carefully observe that these market trends are not merely due to economic factors but are part of a larger geopolitical game.
