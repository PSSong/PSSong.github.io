Recently, large banks have been drawing attention in the financial markets through derivatives trading that bets on the instability of private credit funds. This movement can be interpreted as more than just a part of financial risk management; it can be seen as a strategic attempt to accelerate the centralization of capital. Through the crisis of private credit funds, banks are revealing their intention to strengthen their dominance in the derivatives market and, in doing so, restructure capital flows into a more centralized structure.

This strategy could result in strengthening control within the financial system and weakening the influence of individual investors or small-scale financial institutions. Consequently, the centralization of financial markets is highly likely to further expand the power of large financial institutions and limit market diversity and competition. Therefore, financial market participants must carefully monitor these trends and seek alternatives for the decentralization of capital.
