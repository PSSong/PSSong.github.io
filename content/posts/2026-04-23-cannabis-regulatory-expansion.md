The Trump administration's decision to reclassify cannabis is being interpreted as more than a simple policy change; it is a strategic move to expand the influence of the pharmaceutical industry and create new regulatory domains. This paves the way for pharmaceutical companies to enter the cannabis-based medicine market and provides opportunities to further control the regulatory environment of related industries.

The pharmaceutical industry is seeking new revenue-generating opportunities through the medical use of cannabis. This movement helps pharmaceutical companies strengthen their competitiveness in the existing drug market and establish new regulatory frameworks by investing in the development of new drugs utilizing cannabis components. This contributes not only to the economic interests of the pharmaceutical industry but also to strengthening the close cooperative relationship between the government and the industry.

Ultimately, the expansion of cannabis regulation is a strategic approach for the sustainable growth of the pharmaceutical industry, foreshadowing structural changes in the future medical and pharmaceutical markets.
