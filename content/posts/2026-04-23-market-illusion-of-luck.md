Over the past few days, a perception has been spreading that the market is remaining stable, but there are suggestions that this may be a strategic narrative designed to conceal real crises, such as rising energy prices and supply chain disruptions. This narrative of being in a 'lucky' situation serves to mask fundamental economic problems while simultaneously mitigating the alertness of investors and the general public.

Rising energy prices and supply chain instability are exerting significant pressure on the economy, yet the market narrative is downplaying these issues as temporary and manageable factors. This results in an effect where economic actors delay appropriate responses to crisis situations or underestimate the severity of the crisis.

Consequently, by concealing elements of economic instability, this narrative helps governments and corporations obtain the social consensus necessary to pursue structural changes. Therefore, the current stability of the market may be a temporary phenomenon, and it is crucial to understand the true nature of the crisis hidden behind it.
