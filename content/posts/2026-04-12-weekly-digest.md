## Key Trends This Week
The global situation this week was primarily characterized by intensifying inflationary pressures driven by geopolitical tensions and rising energy prices. In particular, supply chain disruptions caused by the El Niño phenomenon and geopolitical factors, alongside efforts to mask monetary policy failures, have further exacerbated inflation. Rising energy prices are amplifying social unrest, and tensions between Iran and the United States are accelerating energy supply instability, prompting central banks to respond with hawkish monetary policies. This trend has led to the movement of capital into safe-haven assets and an increase in investment in digital assets within the financial markets.

## [Supply Chain Inflation] Section
Supply chain disruptions and inflationary pressures were further worsened by El Niño and geopolitical tensions. On April 9 and 11, central banks and global market forces showed a tendency to simplify inflation issues as energy price volatility. This strategy is interpreted as an attempt to conceal the failures of monetary policy, and as inflationary pressures intensified, the stance of central banks shifted toward becoming more hawkish.

## [Energy and Social Unrest] Section
Rising energy prices are amplifying social unrest, increasing the possibility of large-scale protests and political instability. The collapse of negotiations between the United States and Iran has accelerated energy supply instability and promoted price increases. This has become even more complex as military tensions in the Middle East escalate. This situation is expected to lead to sustained social unrest and political instability.

## [Iran-US Tension] Section
Tensions between Iran and the United States have driven up energy prices, which has been interpreted as a strategy to maintain inflationary pressure. The escalation of tensions on April 9 coincides with efforts to mask monetary policy failures through geopolitical risks.

## [Other Trends]
- **Defense Technology Support**: Amid geopolitical instability, there were movements to defend the stock prices of defense and large corporations through political support from the U.S. government.
- **Bitcoin ETF and Digital Assets**: Amid geopolitical uncertainty, a trend emerged where institutional investors are moving funds into Bitcoin ETFs and digital assets.

## Weekly Conclusion: Looking Ahead to Next Week
The trends of this week suggest that geopolitical tensions and rising energy prices will continue to strengthen inflationary pressures and promote hawkish monetary policies from central banks. These factors are likely to sustain investment in safe-haven assets and the growth of digital assets. Therefore, next week, we must closely observe trends in energy prices and inflation, alongside the possibility of financial market stabilization following such a potential easing of tensions.
