The energy crisis is creating new waves in the global economy. Recent attacks on the energy infrastructure of Iran and Saudi Arabia have served as catalysts, driving up crude oil prices and reigniting inflation. This situation goes beyond mere accidental events and can be interpreted as part of an economic strategy. While central banks are faced with the need to take strong measures, such as raising interest rates to curb inflation, these movements may result in limiting capital liquidity.

At the same time, efforts to avoid the constraints of central bank policies are facilitating the movement of capital into digital assets and the energy sector. In particular, digital assets such as Bitcoin ETFs are emerging as alternatives to evade the instability of the traditional financial system. These strategic movements imply an intention to induce structural changes in the economy through rising energy prices. This can be interpreted as an attempt to restructure capital into a new economic paradigm by bypassing the policy constraints of central banks.

These changes appear to reflect a long-term process of global economic realignment, moving beyond simple economic trends. Therefore, fluctuations in energy prices and rising inflation should be understood as strategic elements driving new flows of capital, rather than mere economic problems.
