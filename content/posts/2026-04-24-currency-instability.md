The challenges facing the Bank of Japan's (BOJ) monetary policy, along with the risk of yen depreciation, are amplifying the instability of the global currency network. Despite the BOJ's continuous quantitative easing policies, the difficulty in balancing inflation management and economic growth is acting as a factor that weakens the credibility of the yen.

This instability is fueling skepticism regarding the role of the Japanese yen in global financial markets, further increasing dependence on other major currencies. In particular, changes in the relationships between major currencies, such as the US dollar and the euro, are influencing global capital flows and further increasing the complexity of the international financial system. The possibility of a BOJ policy failure is not limited solely to the Japanese economy but is amplifying concerns regarding the stability of the global economy.
