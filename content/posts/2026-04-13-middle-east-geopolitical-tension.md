As geopolitical tensions in the Middle East escalate, they are creating significant waves in the energy market. Recent U.S. movements suggest an intention that goes beyond mere regional stabilization, aiming to expand influence over the global economy through rising energy prices. This can be interpreted as a strategy to reignite inflationary pressures by leveraging the instability of the Middle East region. 

In particular, the U.S. move to escalate tensions surrounding its relationship with Iran is directly impacting international oil prices. This situation does more than simply cause energy prices to rise; it is amplifying uncertainty in the global economy. Investors are predicting future energy price volatility through this geopolitical instability, which is acting as a factor that reignites inflationary pressure across various industrial sectors.

However, hidden behind this strategy is an intention by the United States to readjust global economic flows to align with its own interests. As tensions in the Middle East escalate and energy prices rise, the U.S. appears to be attempting to manipulate the international economic environment to suit its objectives, alongside its domestic economic policies. In this context, geopolitical tension in the Middle East is emerging not merely as a regional issue, but as a core variable of the global economy.
