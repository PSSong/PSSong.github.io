The regulatory environment for digital assets is showing a recent shift in direction. Until two days ago, the pace of regulatory tightening seemed to be somewhat easing, but the situation has reversed with recent warnings from the BIS and increased regulation on cryptocurrency exchanges. This change reflects the inevitable adjustment process that occurs as digital assets are integrated into the institutional framework.

This demonstrates the commitment of global financial authorities to enhance the stability and transparency of digital assets, while also suggesting that the digital asset market will likely operate in a more regulated environment in the future. While such regulatory tightening may pose constraints for early investors, it is expected to contribute to increasing the market's reliability and stability in the long term. Therefore, these trends can be interpreted as a significant turning point for the sustainable growth of the digital asset industry.
