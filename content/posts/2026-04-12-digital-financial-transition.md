The IMF and the World Bank are recently accelerating the introduction of Central Bank Digital Currencies (CBDCs) and centralized digital financial systems under the pretext of cybersecurity threats. While highlighting the vulnerabilities of the existing financial system, this also reveals an intention to establish new control and management mechanisms. In particular, although the strengthening of digital financial infrastructure appears to emphasize privacy protection and the transparency of financial transactions, it can, in reality, be interpreted as a move toward expanding the influence of central banks and international organizations within the financial market.

This movement appears to promote the digitalization of the financial system while simultaneously laying the groundwork for nations and international organizations to monitor and control financial transactions more effectively. However, concerns are being raised regarding individual financial freedom and data security during this process, and discussions continue regarding how these changes will affect the transparency and fairness of the financial market.
