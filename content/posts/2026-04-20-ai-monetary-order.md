On April 20, 2026, a new monetary order is being designed alongside the Fed's personnel renewal. The nomination of Kevin Warsh reflects a commitment to introducing a tech-friendly approach to monetary policy. These changes are expected to significantly enhance the efficiency and predictability of monetary policy through the utilization of AI technology. AI will enable more sophisticated and rapid policy adjustments by analyzing macroeconomic data in real-time.

In particular, the strengthening of regulations on stablecoins is interpreted as a crucial step in accelerating the transition to digital currencies. Along with recent warnings from the BIS, the Fed's movement is facilitating the transition toward Central Bank Digital Currencies (CBDC). This reveals an intention to go beyond the digitalization of the existing financial system and to manage the implementation and monitoring of monetary policy more effectively.

These changes herald a structural reorganization of the global financial system, moving beyond mere technological advancement. AI-based monetary policy is expected to reduce economic uncertainty, while the proliferation of digital currencies will play a role in strengthening monetary control. The Fed's strategy is positioning itself as a key element in reshaping the future monetary order.
