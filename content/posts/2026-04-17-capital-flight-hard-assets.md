As geopolitical instability persists, capital is rapidly shifting toward hard assets and cryptocurrencies. This goes beyond a simple risk aversion strategy, signifying a transition toward new means of financial stability and control. Hard assets have traditionally been recognized as means of stability and value preservation, while cryptocurrencies are emerging as a new store of value in the digital age.

This trend suggests that investors are reconsidering their trust in the existing financial system. In particular, cryptocurrencies are demonstrating the potential to serve as decentralized means of value storage and transaction, moving away from centralized financial systems. However, these changes simultaneously increase the complexity of the financial system and raise the need for new regulation and oversight. Therefore, investors and policymakers must closely analyze the long-term implications that these changes in capital flow will bring.
