Analyses are emerging that the Federal Reserve's interest rate decision-making process is becoming increasingly exposed to political pressure. There is a view that recent policy adjustments reflect not only economic factors but also political considerations. This politicization increases the instability of currency values, prompting investors to reallocate capital into real assets that guarantee stable value.

Among real assets, gold, crude oil, and the cryptocurrency Bitcoin are gaining significant attention. These assets are recognized as hedges against the depreciation of traditional currencies and are gaining the trust of investors. In particular, Bitcoin is emerging as an alternative to the existing monetary system due to its scarcity as a digital asset and its decentralized nature.

As the Fed's policy decisions are influenced by politics, the instability of currency values is likely to expand further. This accelerates the movement of capital into real assets and could bring significant changes to economic fund flow patterns in the long term. Investors should carefully monitor these trends and consider portfolio adjustments to prepare for currency depreciation.
