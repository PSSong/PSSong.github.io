Recent observations suggest that issues such as negotiations between the US and Iran and China's AI technology theft are being used as tools to amplify financial market volatility, rather than leading to direct military conflict. These issues attract international attention, increasing the price volatility of specific asset classes, thereby creating a structure where certain investors can reap profits.

In the case of US-Iran negotiations, the prices of the energy sector and assets related to the Middle East region fluctuate significantly depending on the progress and outcome of the negotiations. This can be seen as an instance where geopolitical uncertainty is utilized as a tool for direct profit generation in the financial markets. Similarly, the issue of China's AI technology theft shakes investor sentiment within the technology sector, increasing the stock price volatility of related companies.

Ultimately, the instrumentalization of these geopolitical conflicts aims for the maximization of strategic interests within the financial markets rather than actual warfare. While investors seek opportunities through this volatility, market instability is on a continuous upward trend.
