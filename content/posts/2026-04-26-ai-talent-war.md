April 26, 2026. The movement of Big Tech companies to secure AI talent continues. This is a continuation of the issues that have been ongoing since a day ago, demonstrating that the competition surrounding technological hegemony is intensifying. Beyond simply securing talent, these movements contain strategic elements intended to conceal structural problems within the tech industry and divert attention.

The talent war in the AI field is accelerating technological innovation, but at the same time, it plays a role in strengthening the monopoly structure within the tech industry. Companies seek to expand their market dominance through superiority in AI technology, and in this process, fair access to technology and the diversity of innovation tend to be relatively neglected. This phenomenon hides the structural problems of the tech industry and causes the competition for technological hegemony to focus on the competitive landscape between companies rather than on technological progress.

Ultimately, the AI talent war is playing a role in further deepening the issues of inequality and monopoly within the tech industry beneath the surface of technological innovation.
