Over the past five days, news regarding the impact of the Iran war on the international energy market has been continuously reported. However, there are suggestions that these events may go beyond mere geopolitical conflict and be part of a deeper economic strategy. Observations are emerging that rising energy prices are being utilized to drive inflation, which can be interpreted as a strategic move to promote fundamental changes in the economic structure alongside the restructuring of supply chains.

The direct impact of the war is manifesting as rising energy prices. However, there is a larger picture behind this. Economic experts point out that these price increases may not simply be a result of geopolitical instability, but could be an intentional move by governments and corporations to readjust the economy through inflation. This serves to suppress consumption through rising prices while simultaneously promoting structural changes to increase supply chain efficiency.

In particular, this strategy appears to target not just energy prices, but also seeks a sustainable growth model for the global economy through the overall restructuring of supply chains. Therefore, the aftermath of the Iran war needs to be understood as part of a long-term economic policy, beyond a short-term energy crisis.

In this context, rather than focusing solely on the outcomes of the war, the international community requires a close analysis of how rising energy prices and inflation are transforming the economic structure.
