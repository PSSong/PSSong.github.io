The geopolitical instability in Iran and Hungary is bringing significant changes to global capital flows. This instability is prompting investors to redistribute capital into safe-haven assets and strategic energy assets. In particular, demand for safe-haven assets such as stable government bonds or gold is increasing, and energy assets also tend to maintain or increase their value amidst geopolitical uncertainty.

Financial institutions are actively leveraging these changes in capital flows to rebalance their portfolios. This can be interpreted as an effort to generate returns even amidst geopolitical instability, moving beyond simple risk aversion strategies. Therefore, investors will likely need to carefully monitor changes in capital flows driven by geopolitical factors and adjust their investment strategies accordingly.
