Despite recent controversies regarding flaws in AI technology, the flow of capital and policy remains concentrated on AI hardware and high-performance GPU infrastructure. In particular, tech companies and government agencies are continuing massive investments to advance AI technology, which is accelerating the proliferation of AI-based services.

Central banks are also accelerating the construction of digital financial infrastructure in line with this trend. The establishment of digital financial systems, including the introduction of Central Bank Digital Currencies (CBDC), improves the efficiency and transparency of financial transactions while opening up the possibility of creating new financial services and markets. This shift promotes the digitalization of the financial system alongside technological innovation, moving toward strengthening competitiveness in the future economic environment.

However, new challenges, such as privacy protection and data security issues, are also being raised during this transition process. Therefore, thorough response strategies for these issues are required alongside technological advancement.
