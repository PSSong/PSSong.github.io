Recent large-scale rebalancing movements by pension funds and warnings from Goldman Sachs represent a significant turning point in global financial markets. In response to increasing economic uncertainty, investors are accelerating the rebalancing of portfolios from risky assets to real assets. This shift is interpreted as part of an effort to reduce excessive market risk exposure and enhance stability.

In particular, the increase in investment in real assets is driving heightened interest in gold, real estate, and commodities. This trend appears to be more than just a short-term market adjustment; it seems to be part of a strategic asset allocation in preparation for long-term changes in the economic environment. Consequently, this market rebalancing is expected to have a significant impact on future investment strategies and economic growth prospects.
