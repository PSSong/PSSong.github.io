Economic experts and government authorities have recently shown a tendency to highlight positive economic indicators. This can be interpreted as a strategy to divert public concern regarding stagflation and food crises. By emphasizing positive aspects such as economic growth, employment indicators, and the recovery of certain industries, there is an underlying intention to mitigate overall economic instability.

However, while this approach may provide short-term stability, it also carries the risk of obscuring awareness of structural problems in the long term. The threat of stagflation still exists, and complex issues such as food crises are not easily resolved. Therefore, highlighting positive indicators may serve as a trap that diverts public attention while potentially overlooking the necessity of preparing fundamental measures to address actual problems.

Ultimately, this diversion strategy appears to be an attempt to simplify the complexity of the economic situation, and readers require a balanced understanding of both positive indicators and structural challenges.
