Following the collapse of negotiations between the US and Iran on the 1st, anxiety regarding the energy market is further intensifying. This breakdown is being interpreted as more than a mere diplomatic failure; it is seen as strategic pressure that sustains instability in the energy supply chain. As major oil export routes passing through the Strait of Hormuz once again find themselves at the center of military tension following the failed negotiations, the instability in this region is exerting continuous upward pressure on energy prices.

In particular, as military tensions in this region escalate, the international energy market is becoming increasingly unstable. Energy suppliers are showing a tendency to raise prices to reflect this uncertainty, which is highly likely to lead to rising energy costs worldwide. Furthermore, this situation is prompting investors to reconsider investments in the energy sector and is calling for a review of long-term energy strategies.

The current situation suggests that improving relations between the US and Iran will not be easy, which is expected to maintain ongoing uncertainty in the energy market. Therefore, energy market participants will likely need to pay even closer attention to these geopolitical factors.
