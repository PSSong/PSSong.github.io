Amidst geopolitical instability, a phenomenon is being observed where the stock prices of defense and big tech companies are remaining robust alongside political support. Major companies such as Palantir, Microsoft, and Intel are exhibiting strategic movements to ensure capital flows related to the maintenance of technological hegemony. In particular, the atmosphere of apparent support for former President Trump is playing a role in strengthening investor confidence in these companies.

This reflects an intention to secure stable capital inflows amidst market uncertainty through political support, rather than simply relying on corporate management performance or market demand. Defense companies are presenting possibilities for continuous growth based on demand directly linked to national security, while big tech companies are presenting them through technological innovation and government contracts. This strategy aims to maintain technological hegemony even in times of crisis and to secure capital in preparation for a future economic recovery period.

Consequently, the defense of these companies' stock prices amidst geopolitical instability can be interpreted as part of a complex strategy that combines political support and technological value.
