On April 20, 2026, alongside the Bank for International Settlements' (BIS) warnings regarding the strengthening of stablecoin regulations, the Federal Reserve's personnel reshuffle is being interpreted as a significant turning point in the expansion of digital currency control. This movement is playing a role in accelerating the transition toward Central Bank Digital Currencies (CBDC).

The tightening of stablecoin regulations is part of an effort to ensure stability and transparency in the digital currency market. At the same time, however, it reflects the intention of central banks to strengthen their control over digital currencies. Along with the personnel changes at the Fed, these regulations demonstrate a strategic approach to integrating existing cryptocurrencies and stablecoins into a centralized system.

These changes demonstrate the will of global financial authorities to enhance the effectiveness of monetary policy and strengthen the security and control of the financial system through the proliferation of digital currencies. Consequently, the strengthening of stablecoin regulation is promoting the introduction of CBDCs and playing a role in accelerating the centralization of the digital currency order.
