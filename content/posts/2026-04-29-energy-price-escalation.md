Recently, former President Trump's hardline stance toward Iran and the internal division within OPEC have been causing significant waves in the energy market. Trump's threats against Iran are shaking the stability of the Middle East region and amplifying uncertainty in the oil supply. This geopolitical tension is acting as a catalyst for rising crude oil prices, which in turn serves as a factor reigniting inflation in the global economy.

Within OPEC, the framework of cooperation is showing signs of instability as differences in national interests become more prominent. This division makes the consistent management of oil production difficult and adds uncertainty to the market. This situation suggests the possibility of a sustained rise in oil prices to investors and corporations, prompting a reassessment of investment strategies in the energy sector.

However, there is room to interpret these movements as more than just a pursuit of economic profit; they could be seen as a means to achieve the strategic goals of certain nations. Geopolitical tensions driving up energy prices can create situations where inflation is difficult to control, thereby exerting economic pressure. Therefore, we must remain vigilant regarding the possibility that deeper political intentions, beyond simple market factors, may be hidden behind the rise in energy prices.
