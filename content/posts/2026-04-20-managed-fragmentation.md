April 20, 2026, the global economy is exhibiting new patterns of fragmentation amidst the strategic movements of the United States. As tensions between Iran and Israel have recently escalated, an increase in energy prices is being predicted. This suggests that the United States is utilizing geopolitical conflicts as a tool to maintain and strengthen its energy hegemony. It can be inferred that the US intends to create instability in the energy market through these conflicts to further solidify its own energy dominance.

Furthermore, the United States is pursuing a strategy to disperse global economic dependence through the decentralization of supply chains. Efforts are visible to reduce excessive dependence on specific countries or regions by diversifying energy and raw material supply chains through third countries such as Brazil. This movement appears to contribute not only to securing economic stability but also to expanding geopolitical influence.

In terms of the monetary system, preparations are underway for the introduction of Central Bank Digital Currencies (CBDC). This reflects the intention to strengthen control over digital currencies and further solidify US leadership within the global financial system. While accelerating the fragmentation of the global economy, these changes are creating a structure where the United States plays a leading role at its center.

These strategic movements reflect a complex US plan to secure long-term geopolitical advantage beyond mere short-term economic gains. The fragmentation of the global economy is proceeding under US leadership, signaling a fundamental shift in the international economic order.
