Tensions are escalating in the international energy market as Iran attempts to leverage the strategic position of the Strait of Hormuz to initiate a blockade. As a major route for global oil transport, Iran's actions immediately trigger rises in global energy prices, with the impact extending beyond simple price fluctuations to lead toward structural inflation. 

Iran's strategy can be interpreted as more than just exerting economic pressure; it serves as a means to convey a political message to the international community. In particular, recent U.S. movements regarding the strengthening of sanctions appear to be a response to Iran's actions, through which the U.S. is demonstrating its commitment to strengthening energy security. 

However, the repetition of such sanctions and blockade threats has the potential to amplify instability in the global energy supply chain in the long term. This will drive nations to focus more on alternative energy sources and supply chain diversification, which could ultimately exert sustained upward pressure on energy prices. Therefore, the political motives behind Iran's actions and the resulting economic repercussions are aspects that must be more closely observed on the international stage.
