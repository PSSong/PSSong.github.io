The recently announced tariff refund policy focuses on easing the cost burden of large retailers and mitigating upward pressure on consumer prices. While these measures appear on the surface to be aimed at relieving economic burdens, they can be interpreted as a strategic move to temporarily conceal economic instability.

In the short term, tariff refunds can have the effect of suppressing consumer price increases and stimulating economic activity. However, in the long term, they are highly likely to fail to address the fundamental causes of inflation and instead serve to temporarily mask economic instability by adjusting market expectations. This appears to be an approach focused on maintaining political stability rather than providing a fundamental solution to economic problems.
