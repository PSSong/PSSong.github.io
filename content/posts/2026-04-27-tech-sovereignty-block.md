The Chinese government's recent moves to strengthen technology regulations reflect one facet of the global competition for technological hegemony. The incident where Meta's plan to acquire services within China was blocked clearly demonstrates China's determination to protect its domestic technological ecosystem from external influence. These measures can be interpreted as more than just limiting corporate market entry; they are strategic decisions aimed at securing national sovereignty in future technology sectors, such as AI.

On a global scale, there is a high possibility that these movements will stimulate policies for strengthening technological sovereignty in other nations. The tightening of technology regulations reflects China's attempt to reshape international technological standards and norms to align with its own interests, which is expected to serve as a catalyst for rebalancing the global technological ecosystem. Therefore, China's actions are observed to play a crucial role in forming a new paradigm in international technology policy, moving beyond mere changes in domestic policy.
