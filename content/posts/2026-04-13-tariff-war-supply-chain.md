As the tariff war between the United States and China continues, global supply chains are being restructured. Through tariffs on China, the United States is intensifying economic pressure while simultaneously revealing its intention to enhance the competitiveness of its domestic manufacturing. This strategy goes beyond simply resolving trade imbalances; it also encompasses the objective of strengthening hegemony over energy and resources through the weaponization of supply chains.

The restructuring of supply chains is progressing in a direction that reduces dependence on China across various industrial sectors and instead strengthens cooperation with allies. While these changes may lead to increased costs and decreased productivity in the short term, they can be interpreted as an attempt by the United States to further solidify its leadership in the global economy in the long term. In particular, the stable procurement of energy resources and the diversification of supply chains are closely aligned with U.S. geopolitical goals.

Ultimately, the US-China tariff war is acting as a factor that triggers the reorganization of the global economic order beyond a mere economic conflict, and this includes the process of redefining the strategic positions of energy hegemony and manufacturing.
