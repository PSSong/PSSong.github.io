The continuous rise in energy prices is heightening social discontent, thereby increasing the risk of causing political instability. The recent fuel protests in Ireland serve as one example demonstrating how rising energy prices can be directly linked to social unrest. These protests can be interpreted as an expression of social discontent and distrust in the government's policy response capabilities, going beyond mere economic hardship.

Rising energy prices directly pressure the public's standard of living by limiting access to essential elements of daily life. This pressure amplifies social tensions and is highly likely to eventually lead to large-scale riots. In particular, this instability can become even more severe when economic inequality and political discontent act in combination.

This situation emphasizes the need for governments to readjust their energy and social welfare policies. The social instability caused by rising energy prices can act as a factor that amplifies geopolitical tensions and political unrest, moving beyond a simple economic issue. Therefore, governments and the international community must establish strategies to proactively manage and respond to such social instability.
