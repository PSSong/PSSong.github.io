The instability of energy supply is maximizing the profits of energy companies and promoting hedging strategies for massive capital to expand market share during crisis situations. These strategies seek stable returns by leveraging energy price volatility, while simultaneously strengthening supply chain diversification and risk management in times of crisis.

Massive capital is dispersing risks during crises through the procurement and diversification of energy resources. This reflects an intention to secure long-term dominance in the energy market, moving beyond simple short-term profit-seeking. In particular, in the process of rebalancing between renewable energy and traditional fossil fuels, massive capital is employing various hedging strategies, demonstrating efforts to transform market uncertainty into opportunity.

These movements are accelerating structural changes in the energy market and are expected to exert a significant influence on future energy policies and investment directions.
