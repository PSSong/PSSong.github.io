The tightening of regulations on the cryptocurrency market, particularly policies such as the prohibition of duplicate listings, is recently shifting its direction from an 'escalation' stance to 'de-escalation.' While this change is presented under the pretext of enhancing transparency, it can be interpreted as a form of attempt to control capital flows within the institutional framework.

Previously, regulatory tightening was perceived as a factor that amplified instability in the cryptocurrency market, but the recent policy shift is interpreted as part of an effort toward market stabilization. This implies an intention to reduce market uncertainty by providing a clearer regulatory environment for cryptocurrency investors and companies. At the same time, however, such regulations have the potential to act in a direction that limits the free flow of cryptocurrencies and strengthens control within the institutional framework.

The background of this transition reflects the will of global financial authorities for more systematic management of the cryptocurrency market. This change appears to seek a balance between the sustainable growth of the cryptocurrency industry and the maintenance of financial system stability.
