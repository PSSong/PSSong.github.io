April 11, 2026. A strategy is underway to simplify the complex causes of inflation into energy price volatility, which is being utilized to conceal the failures of monetary policy and dilute public perception. As recent geopolitical crises and rising energy prices are emphasized as the primary drivers of inflationary pressure, discussions regarding the limitations of monetary policy and structural economic issues are relatively decreasing.

This trend, which has continued since the 1st, focuses the inflation narrative on the volatility of energy prices, serving as a way to evade criticism regarding the effectiveness of monetary policy. By concentrating public attention on energy prices and geopolitical factors, this approach effectively dilates awareness of monetary policy failures or deeper economic problems.

Consequently, this manipulation of the narrative reflects an intention to achieve the strategic goal of easing inflationary pressure through the stabilization of energy prices, while simultaneously masking the limitations of monetary policy. However, it should be noted that such simplified narratives may have limitations in addressing the complex problems of the economy in the long term.
