Recently, the brain drain in the field of artificial intelligence (AI) has emerged as a serious challenge for established software companies. This talent outflow goes beyond a simple movement of personnel; it is reshaping the competitive landscape surrounding technological superiority. As leading companies such as OpenAI absorb talent, existing firms are facing situations where they fall behind in the speed of innovation and technological development capabilities.

This leads to the formation of a new value chain. Companies focusing on AI infrastructure-related services, data management, and algorithm development are emerging. This shift signifies more than just competition over technological advantage; it represents a structural reorganization of the industry. Established software companies are showing movements to adapt to this rapidly changing environment by internalizing AI technology or through partnerships.

Ultimately, the AI talent war can be interpreted as a process of redefining the methods of value creation in the future economy, moving beyond the competition for leadership in technological innovation. This will lead to efforts by companies to explore new business models related to AI infrastructure and secure competitiveness through them.
