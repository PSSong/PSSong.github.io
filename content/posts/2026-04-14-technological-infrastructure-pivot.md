Recent moves to expand satellite communication infrastructure, such as Amazon's acquisition of Globalstar, are interpreted as a strategic approach to prepare for geopolitical instability, going beyond mere technological investment. The expansion of satellite communication networks ensures the stable and secure transmission of data and plays a role in compensating for the vulnerabilities of existing communication infrastructure, particularly during geopolitical conflicts.

These movements are part of an effort to secure data sovereignty and communication sovereignty amidst intensifying competition between nations. Strengthening satellite communication infrastructure contributes to maintaining regional connectivity and enhancing the ability to control the flow of information between countries. Therefore, this can be viewed as a strategic reorganization for global technological hegemony and geopolitical stability, moving beyond simple technological progress.
