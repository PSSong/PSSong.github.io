The instability in the energy market that has continued since the 4th can be interpreted as a result of the complex entanglement of former President Trump's energy policies and the geopolitical situation in Iran. While Trump's policies were directed toward promoting energy production and increasing U.S. energy independence, they are simultaneously acting as a factor causing supply instability in the global energy market.

In particular, geopolitical tensions related to Iran are acting as a factor that further expands energy price volatility. The sanctions from the Trump administration and current diplomatic uncertainties are amplifying concerns regarding energy supply in the Middle East, which consequently plays a role in sustaining global inflationary pressures. This situation reflects attempts to use energy prices as a political tool, exerting continuous pressure on the global economy.
