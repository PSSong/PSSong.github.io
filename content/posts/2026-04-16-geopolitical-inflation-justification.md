Amid escalating geopolitical tensions, observations are being made that the conflict between Iran and Israel is being utilized as a tool to reignite inflationary pressures. This crisis situation amplifies economic uncertainty and provides the necessary justification for central banks to maintain or strengthen interest rate policies aimed at curbing inflation. 

Economic factors are deeply embedded in the background of this conflict. Central banks were already implementing tight monetary policies to curb high inflation rates. However, as the possibility of geopolitical instability causing additional energy price hikes and supply chain disruptions increases, this crisis is being used as a reason for the continuous management of inflation. 

This does not merely reflect economic factors but can also be interpreted within a political context. Governments and central banks may have an underlying intention to mitigate criticism regarding changes in economic policy and strengthen the perception that current tight policies are necessary through these crisis situations. However, caution is required, as such strategies could have a negative impact on economic recovery and growth in the long term.
