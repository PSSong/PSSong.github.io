The strong performance of major global banks reaffirms the phenomenon of capital concentration in financial markets. The robust results of key financial institutions, such as JPMorgan and Citigroup, demonstrate their ability for stable capital management and risk diversification even in times of crisis, reflecting the tendency of investors to concentrate capital into safe assets within the financial hegemony.

This concentration of capital results in the strengthening of dominance within the financial system. As large banks maintain solid performance even during crises, market participants further strengthen their trust in these institutions, which plays a role in further solidifying the structure of financial hegemony. Therefore, this trend, along with structural changes in financial markets, is expected to have a significant impact on future capital allocation patterns.
