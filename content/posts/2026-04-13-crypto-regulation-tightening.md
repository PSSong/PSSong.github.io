The movement toward strengthening virtual asset regulations, including the Korea Financial Intelligence Unit's (FIU) suspension of Coinone's operations, reflects an intention to suppress the decentralization of capital amidst macroeconomic uncertainty. While these regulatory tightenings aim to mitigate instability in the virtual asset market, a deeper look reveals they can be interpreted as an attempt to strengthen the control of the financial system.

The FIU's measures are part of an effort to increase the transparency and stability of virtual asset transactions, but they are simultaneously linked to the expansion of financial control systems, such as the introduction of Central Bank Digital Currencies (CBDC). These changes imply an objective to suppress the tendency of individual investors and corporations to exit the financial system through virtual assets. 

Ultimately, the strengthening of regulations appears to be an attempt to further control capital flows within the traditional financial system while simultaneously limiting the growth of the virtual asset market. This trend is highly likely to act as a factor that reinforces the tendency toward centralization in the global financial market.
