On April 26, 2026, US geopolitical movements are fundamentally altering the global economic structure. Recent military tensions with Iran and intensified sanctions against China are being interpreted as more than mere diplomatic responses; they are part of a US-led strategy of economic fragmentation. This strategy aims to dismantle existing global supply chains and reallocate capital toward a US-centric complex of technology, defense, and finance.

The core of this strategy lies in building new economic networks through strengthened cooperation with allies. In particular, achieving self-sufficiency in defense and advanced technologies plays a role in reinforcing stability within the US-led economic system. However, these movements are increasing global economic uncertainty, leading various nations to either integrate into the US-centric economic system or seek new alliances to counter it.

Ultimately, this economic fragmentation signifies more than just an economic issue; it represents a redistribution of geopolitical power and is expected to have a profound impact on future international relations and the economic order.
