Close cooperation between the European Central Bank (ECB), the International Monetary Fund (IMF), and the Bank for International Settlements (BIS) suggests a coordinated monetary policy response in preparation for global economic crises. By strengthening information sharing and joint response strategies among central banks, this cooperation is laying the foundation to effectively deal with future economic uncertainties.

This coordinated approach goes beyond simply maintaining economic stability; it focuses on establishing a framework that enables the management of global economic uncertainty and facilitates rapid cooperation during crisis situations. This movement of central banks toward a common goal is evaluated as a significant step in strengthening the stability and resilience of the global economy.
