JPMorgan and Netflix's recent earnings announcements are playing a role in highlighting the outward growth of corporations and distracting investors. However, these announcements can be interpreted as a 'cognitive dilution' strategy to mask the private credit crisis within the bond market and the instability of corporate debt structures. The risks in the private credit market still persist, and such earnings announcements are being utilized to obscure the actual vulnerabilities of the financial system.

Investors must not be misled by these earnings announcements and need to closely analyze the actual financial health and debt structures of companies. In particular, since the instability of the private credit market has a significant impact on the entire financial system, it is crucial not to overlook it. In this context, more rigorous supervision is required to ensure financial market transparency and investor protection.
