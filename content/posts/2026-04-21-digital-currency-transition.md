The strengthening of regulations on Central Bank Digital Currencies (CBDC) and stablecoins, led by the Bank for International Settlements (BIS) and various central banks, reflects a centralization strategy for the digital economy. Analyses suggest that these movements are moving toward placing private-sector digital assets under the control of central governments or institutions.

The introduction of CBDCs enhances the ability of the state to directly manage and control digital transactions. While this increases the efficiency of the financial system, it also carries the potential to expand surveillance and control over individual financial activities. Regulations on stablecoins can be understood in a similar context. Under the pretext of enhancing stability and transparency, the regulation of private stablecoins is progressing toward ultimately placing them under the control of central banks or governments.

This centralization strategy reflects the intention to expand the economic influence of the state alongside the development of the digital economy. The digitalization of the global financial system provides opportunities for states and central banks to manage and control the flow of digital assets more effectively. Therefore, these changes offer significant implications for the role of the state and the power structure in the future of the digital economy.
