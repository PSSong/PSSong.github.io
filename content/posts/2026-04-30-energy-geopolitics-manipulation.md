The escalation of tensions in the Middle East and the fluctuations in Brent crude oil prices over the past three days have once again highlighted the United States' energy geopolitical strategy. Trump's recent remarks regarding Iran go beyond mere political offensive; they reflect an intention to maintain a pivotal role in controlling energy supplies in the Middle East. Along with these remarks, the increase in oil price volatility can be interpreted as a strategic move by the U.S. to solidify its influence in the energy market by utilizing geopolitical crises.

In particular, the instability of energy supplies in the Middle East has a significant impact on the global economy, providing the United States with an opportunity to further strengthen its hegemony in the energy market. This strategy implies an intention to go beyond simply raising energy prices; it aims to reconfirm the United States' position in the international energy market and restructure hegemony by leveraging the energy dependence of competing nations.

Therefore, the recent Middle East tensions and oil price fluctuations appear to be linked to the strategic goals of the United States, moving beyond simple economic factors. In this context, energy geopolitical crises are being utilized as important tools for the United States to maintain and expand its global energy hegemony.
