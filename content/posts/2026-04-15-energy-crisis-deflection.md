Recently, there has been a trend toward framing the threat of a blockade in the Strait of Hormuz as Iranian aggression. This strategy is an attempt to attribute the instability of energy supplies to Iran, which is being used to justify the global energy crisis. This focuses the international community's attention on Iran's actions, obscuring the structural causes and the broader geopolitical context of the energy crisis.

By positioning Iran as the target of blame, a tendency arises to focus on short-term responses rather than fundamental solutions to the energy crisis. This can lead to efforts to restrict Iran's actions through international pressure and sanctions, which ultimately risks prolonging instability in the energy market.

Therefore, this reframing reflects an intention to manage market anxiety by shifting responsibility to a specific nation instead of addressing the intrinsic problems of the energy crisis. This provides important implications for understanding the complexities of international energy policy.
