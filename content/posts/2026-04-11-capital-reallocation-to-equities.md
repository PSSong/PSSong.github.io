The resolution of geopolitical crises is expected to promote capital inflow into risk assets alongside the stabilization of energy prices. In particular, an increase in capital flow toward the S&P 500 and the defense/big tech sectors is being observed. If recent mediation efforts and crisis management are successful, market participants are highly likely to expand their investments in risk assets alongside stable energy prices.

This trend suggests that the market is interpreting the reduction in geopolitical uncertainty positively. The stabilization of energy prices alleviates inflationary pressures, thereby creating an environment where investors can afford to take more risks. Defense and big tech companies are further strengthening their appeal as risk assets, driven by technological innovation and stable government contracts.

Therefore, if the narrative of geopolitical resolution persists, capital is expected to concentrate even more heavily into risk assets that offer prospects for stable growth and returns.
