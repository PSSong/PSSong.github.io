The geopolitical provocations by Iran and North Korea, which have continued since the 1st, are causing ongoing instability in the energy market. This state of tension is amplifying uncertainty regarding oil and gas supply chains, further increasing the volatility of energy prices. In particular, instability in the Middle East directly impacts the global energy supply chain, leading to ripple effects across the global economy.

Central banks are further emphasizing the necessity of introducing digital control systems through these crisis situations. The volatility of energy prices and the instability of supply chains are serving as catalysts for central banks to seek the establishment of more effective crisis management systems utilizing digital technology. This goes beyond mere crisis response, moving toward securing more flexible and rapid policy adjustment capabilities in preparation for future geopolitical instability.

In this context, energy market volatility can be interpreted as an opportunity to seek new policy directions at the intersection of geopolitical strategy and technological innovation, transcending simple economic factors.
