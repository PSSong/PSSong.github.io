The large-scale bad loan losses faced by major US banks are shaking the very foundation of the financial system. This crisis extends beyond mere economic loss, raising fundamental questions regarding the reliability of the existing financial regime. As the losses of these banks are disclosed, anxiety among investors and consumers is intensifying, triggering a crisis of confidence across the entire financial market.

However, amidst this turmoil, central banks are facilitating a transition toward new control mechanisms. These central banks are reviewing existing regulatory frameworks and moving toward the implementation of more precise risk management systems through digitalization and data analysis. This shift goes beyond simple crisis response, aiming toward a future where the financial system can operate with greater resilience and transparency.

This transition process heralds structural changes within the financial industry and is expected to have a significant impact on the future delivery of financial services and the regulatory environment. While the restructuring of the financial system may cause short-term instability, it could ultimately serve as an opportunity to establish a more stable and efficient system in the long run.
