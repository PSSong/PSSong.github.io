The Trump administration is making moves to create an optimistic atmosphere in the market through a declaration that it has concluded its conflict with Iran. This announcement provides immediate stability and serves to ease the vigilance of investors. However, behind this narrative, there may be an intention to conceal the potential risks of an energy crisis.

This strategy, which emphasizes the resolution of conflict with Iran, instills hope for international oil price stability and economic recovery in the short term. However, it appears to be masking fundamental issues related to the instability of energy supply. In particular, despite the continued existence of geopolitical instability in the Middle East, these issues are effectively being pushed away from the market's primary focus.

Such a shift in framing has the potential to cause investors to lose their sense of alertness regarding long-term energy crisis risks. Therefore, market participants must carefully analyze the complex reality hidden behind these optimistic messages. This could serve as an opportunity to re-evaluate not merely the end of a war, but the structural problems of the energy market and the subsequent potential risks.
